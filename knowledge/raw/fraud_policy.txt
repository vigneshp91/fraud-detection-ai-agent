FRAUD DETECTION POLICY — RISK SCORING GUIDELINES
Version 2.0 | Financial Fraud Operations

==========================================================
1. RISK SCORE THRESHOLDS
==========================================================

Score 0–25   → LOW RISK    → Recommendation: APPROVE
Score 26–60  → MEDIUM RISK → Recommendation: REVIEW
Score 61–85  → HIGH RISK   → Recommendation: BLOCK + Escalate
Score 86–100 → CRITICAL    → Recommendation: BLOCK + Escalate immediately

Any transaction scoring >= 61 MUST be escalated to the human fraud review team.

==========================================================
2. HIGH-RISK INDICATORS (add 20–30 points each)
==========================================================

- Transaction at 2:00 AM – 5:00 AM local time (late-night anomaly)
- Location is a high-fraud geography: Nigeria, Romania, Belarus, Ukraine (non-travel context)
- Transaction amount > $1,000 with no prior large-spend history for this user
- Merchant categorized as "unknown" or missing merchant name
- Online category purchase from a foreign IP or atypical location
- More than 3 fraud flags in the user's last 30 transactions

==========================================================
3. MEDIUM-RISK INDICATORS (add 10–15 points each)
==========================================================

- Transaction amount 2x above the user's historical average
- New location not seen in the user's last 30 transactions
- Electronics purchase over $300 without prior electronics history
- ATM withdrawal in a foreign city
- Transaction in a category with zero prior history for this user

==========================================================
4. LOW-RISK INDICATORS (subtract 5–10 points)
==========================================================

- Merchant is a known national chain (Starbucks, McDonald's, Walmart, Amazon)
- Transaction location matches user's most frequent city
- Amount is within 1 standard deviation of user's average spend
- Category is the user's top-3 most used categories
- Daytime transaction (9 AM – 7 PM)

==========================================================
5. AUTOMATIC BLOCK RULES
==========================================================

- Any transaction from a country with a travel advisory AND amount > $500
- Velocity rule: more than 5 transactions in 30 minutes from different locations
- Duplicate transaction: same amount, merchant, and user within 10 minutes
- Card-present transaction while card is flagged as lost or stolen

==========================================================
6. ESCALATION PROTOCOL
==========================================================

When escalating, the fraud review team requires:
- Transaction ID
- Specific risk factors that triggered escalation
- Preliminary risk score
- User's historical fraud flag count

All escalations are logged and reviewed within 4 business hours.
A human analyst can override the agent recommendation; that override
is recorded as RLHF feedback for future model calibration.

==========================================================
7. PII AND DATA HANDLING
==========================================================

- Full card numbers must never appear in agent reasoning or logs
- User names are acceptable in internal logs but not in output reports
- Location at city/country level is permitted; street address is not
- All agent outputs are PII-scrubbed before storage

==========================================================
8. KNOWN FALSE POSITIVE PATTERNS
==========================================================

- Electronics retailers (Apple Store, Best Buy) routinely trigger medium risk
  for first-time purchasers — verify with user history before blocking
- Travel transactions naturally appear in unusual locations — check for
  prior flight/hotel purchases in the same time window
- Online gaming platforms generate repeated small charges that may look
  like velocity fraud — category context matters
